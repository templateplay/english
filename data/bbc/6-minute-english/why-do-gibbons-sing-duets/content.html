What are gibbons singing about?

Gibbons belong to a group of animals called primates. They are small apes that live in South East Asia. When they communicate with each other they sound like they are singing – and some sing in duets – but why?

Listen to Rob and Neil discussing screeching monkeys, the songs that gibbons sing and the evolution of human language whilst teaching some related vocabulary.

This week's question

How far can a gibbon's voice travel through the forest? Is it…

a) 500m

b) 1km

c) 5km?

Vocabulary

gibbons
small apes that live in Southeast Asia

primates
animals belonging to the same group as humans, which includes monkeys and apes

duets
songs sung by two people

a close shave
a dangerous or difficult situation you just manage to avoid

screeching
making a loud, high and unpleasant sound

face-off
argument or fight

evocative
brings strong feelings or memories to mind

cacophony
mix of loud noises, which often sound out of tune

territory
an area of land

alert
warn

predators
animals that kills and eats other animals

ancestor
an animal – or human – from the past that a modern animal or human has descended from

evolutionary
a gradual process of change or development
