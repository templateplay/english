Why do people often say one thing and do another? Nobody likes a hypocrite, and it's often used as an insult. But is it avoidable? Alice and Rob ask how far hypocrisy is actually part of who we are.

This week's question

What did the Ancient Greek word ‘hypocrite' originally mean? Was it…

a) actor

b) politician

c) horse

You can hear the correct answer at the end of the programme.

Vocabulary

hypocrite
 somebody who says one thing and does another

inconsistent
 changeable

harsh
stern and unkind

compassionate
showing sympathy and concern

catch somebody out
discover somebody has made a mistake

morally indignant
being angry about something according to principles of right and wrong

incongruous
 not in agreement with

pernicious
 deadly or destructive

go viral
 spread very quickly (via the internet)
