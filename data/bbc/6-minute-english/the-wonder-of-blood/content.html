What part of our body have scientists discovered can heal and help us?

For centuries, people have known that human blood is a remarkable substance. But they never really knew just how complex it was. Modern science is now showing us all the amazing things it does. Rob and Finn talk about how detailed research into blood's properties has given us all sorts of new ways to heal our bodies and improve the quality of our lives.

This week's question

If you laid out all the blood vessels in an adult body end to end how long would they be?

a) 30,000 miles

b) 100,000 miles

c) 200,000 miles

Listen to the programme to find out the answer.

a medical procedure where an amount of blood is put into a human

cholesterol-high
a lot of a substance that contains fat and is found in the body tissue and blood of all animals

veins, arteries
tubes that carry blood from the heart and around the body

clotting
forming a solid lump

accelerate
go faster

treated
changed or transformed

probed
investigated

adapt
change in an appropriate way
